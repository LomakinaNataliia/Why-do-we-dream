What is sleep debt?




If you routinely lose sleep or choose to sleep less than needed, the sleep loss adds up. The total sleep lost is called your sleep debt. For example, if you lose 2 hours of sleep each night, you'll have a sleep debt of 14 hours after a week.

Does sleep help your brain work properly?




Sleep deprivation might lead to sleep deficiency that alters activity in some parts of the brain. If you are sleep deficient, you may have trouble making decisions, solving problems, controlling your emotions and behavior, and coping with change. Sleep deficiency also has been linked to depression, suicide, and risk-taking behavior. A study on medical interns provides a good example. Interns on a traditional schedule with extended work hours of more than 24 hours made 36% more serious medical errors than interns on a schedule that allowed more sleep. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15509817/ On the other hand, good sleep has been shown to improve problem-solving skills and enhance the memory performance of both children and adults. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12421655/ https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25052368/ https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19379769/ Studies show that a good night's sleep improves learning. Whether you're learning math, how to play the piano, how to perfect your golf swing, or how to drive a car, sleep helps enhance your learning and problem-solving skills. Sleep also helps you pay attention, make decisions, and be creative.
